# AMD Expands Ryzen AI Embedded P100 Family With 8 to 12 Core Parts

AMD expanded the Ryzen AI Embedded P100 family with higher-end 8-core, 10-core, and 12-core processors. These new parts complement the 4-core and 6-core models that were introduced earlier, and they continue to target automotive, industrial, and other non-PC edge devices that need longer support lifecycles.

![Ryzen AI Embedded hero image](https://www.servethehome.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/Ryzen-AI-Embedded-Hero.jpg "Ryzen AI Embedded family")

ServeTheHome describes the launch as part of a broader refresh of AMD’s Ryzen Embedded lineup around Zen 5-era silicon. Compared with the earlier Ryzen Embedded 8000 generation, the new parts are positioned as delivering clear improvements in CPU, GPU, and NPU performance.

![Ryzen AI Embedded P100 8 to 12 core overview](https://www.servethehome.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/Ryzen-AI-Embedded-P100-8-12c-Overview.png "Ryzen AI Embedded P100 overview")

## Ryzen AI Embedded P100: now up to 12 Zen 5 CPU cores and 16 GPU CUs

AMD is effectively filling out the stack from the bottom up: it launched the low-end parts first, is now adding mid-range 8-core to 12-core parts, and plans a future X100 series that will go as high as 16 CPU cores.

The lower-end P100 chips are based on Krackan Point, while these new higher-performance P100 parts bring Strix Point into the industrial and embedded market.

- Embedded 8000: Zen 4 CPU cores, RDNA 2 graphics, first-generation XDNA NPU, based on Phoenix silicon.
- Ryzen AI Embedded P100: Zen 5 CPU cores, RDNA 3.5 graphics, XDNA 2 NPU, based on Krackan Point and Strix Point.
- Ryzen AI Embedded X100: Zen 5 CPU cores, RDNA 3.5 graphics, XDNA 2 NPU, based on Strix Halo.

The article says the new higher-end P100 parts bring roughly the same architecture as the first wave, but with much more compute: up to twice the CPU performance and up to four times the GPU performance because of the larger core counts.

These chips are meant for more demanding embedded workloads than the 4-core and 6-core parts can handle. AMD highlights industrial automation, physical AI and robotics, healthcare, and some professional broadcast use cases.

![Ryzen AI Embedded P100 SKU table](https://www.servethehome.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/Ryzen-AI-Embedded-P100-SKUs.png "Ryzen AI Embedded P100 SKUs")

AMD introduced six new SKUs in total: three standard models with 8, 10, and 12 CPU cores, plus industrial-temperature variants of those same chips that are qualified down to -40°C.

ServeTheHome notes that AMD does not explicitly separate full Zen 5 from compact Zen 5c cores in its public marketing, but these parts use a mixture of both. The top 12-core SKU reportedly combines 4 Zen 5 cores with 8 Zen 5c cores.

The GPU also scales up sharply. Earlier low-end P100 chips topped out at four graphics compute units, while the new higher-end parts start at twelve CUs and reach sixteen CUs on the top models. That matters both for graphics-heavy embedded devices and for AI workloads that can use more local acceleration.

Platform features otherwise remain shared across the family. Vendors can use either LPDDR5X-8533 memory or DDR5-5600 memory. The nominal TDP remains 28 W, with a configurable range from 15 W to 54 W, and non-automotive SKUs support two USB4 ports.

![Ryzen AI Embedded P100 reference board](https://www.servethehome.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/Ryzen-AI-Embedded-P100-Reference-Board.png "AMD reference board for P100 development")

AMD also previewed a pre-validated reference board that will ship later in the year. The board includes DDR5 SO-DIMM slots, a PCIe x4 slot, USB, DisplayPort, and the kind of general-purpose I/O needed to bring embedded hardware and software projects up quickly.

## Final words

According to AMD, the new processors are already sampling to customers and their documentation and tools are already available.

Full production is scheduled for the third quarter of 2026, one quarter after the lower-end chips. The reference boards are expected to become available in the second half of the year.
